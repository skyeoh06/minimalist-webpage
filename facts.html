Nutrition Facts

Artificial Trans Fats Are Unsuitable for Human Consumption.

Foods with less than 0.5 grams of trans fats per serving may be labeled as having 0 grams.

You Don't Need to Eat Every 2–3 Hours.

Simply eat when you're hungry and be sure to choose healthy and nutritious foods.

Take News Headlines With a Grain of Salt.

When unexpected findings come out, media people and even scientists often try to draw parallels to true scientific discovery— like finding a new planet in the solar system.

But it’s hard to disprove 40 years of nutritional epidemiology with a single study.

Meat Doesn’t Rot in Your Colon.

Most of the fats, proteins, and nutrients are then absorbed by your body.

While small amounts of protein and fat may escape digestion in healthy people, there is not much left to rot in your colon.

Eggs Are One of the Healthiest Foods You Can Eat.

Studies Show that eggs does not have effect on heart disease and blood cholestrol.

Sugary Drinks Are the Most Fattening Product in the Modern Diet.

Your Brain doesn't register liquid sugar calories, making you eat more calories overall.

Low-Fat Doesn’t Mean Healthy.

Yet, because foods tend to taste worse without the fat, manufacturers added sugar and other additives instead.

Fruit Juice Isn’t That Different From Sugary Soft Drinks.

As juice offers no chewing resistance and negligible amounts of fiber, it’s very easy to consume a lot of sugar.

A single cup (240 ml) of orange juice contains just as much sugar as 2 whole oranges.

Feeding Your Gut Bacteria Is Critical

People are really only about 10% human — the bacteria in your intestine, known as the gut flora, outnumber your human cells 10 to 1.

Research has shown that the types and number of these bacteria can have profound implications for human health — affecting everything from body weight to brain function.

Cholesterol Isn’t the Enemy.

The main determinant for heart disease risk is the type of lipoproteins that carry cholesterol around — not cholesterol itself.

Weight Loss Supplements Rarely Work.

Even for the few that work — like glucomannan — the effect is too small to really make a noticeable difference.

Health Is About More Than Your Weight.

It appears that the area where fat builds up is important. The fat in your abdominal cavity (belly fat) is associated with metabolic problems, while the fat under your skin is mostly a cosmetic problem.

Reducing belly fat should be a priority for health improvement.

Calories Count — But You Don't Necessarily Need to Count Them.

For example, eating more protein has been shown to lead to automatic calorie restriction and significant weight loss — without deliberately restricting calories

People With Type 2 Diabetes Shouldn’t Follow a High-Carb Diet.

If anyone benefits from a low-carb diet, it is people with diabetes.

In one study, following a low-carb diet for only 6 months allowed 95.2% of participants to reduce or eliminate their blood sugar medication.

Neither Fat nor Carbs Make You Fat.

As with almost everything in nutrition science, the issue depends on the context.

Both fat and carbs can be fattening — it all depends on the rest of your diet and your overall lifestyle.

Junk Food Can Be Addictive.

These days, food engineers have found ways to make food so rewarding that your brain gets flooded with dopamine.

Never Trust Health Claims on Packaging.

They do this by adding misleading labels like "whole-grain" or "low-fat."

If the packaging of a food tells you it’s healthy, chances are it isn’t.

Certain Vegetable Oils Should Be Avoided.

Certain vegetable oils — like sunflower, soybean, and corn oil — contain large amounts of omega-6 fatty acids.

Studies suggest that a high intake of omega-6 fatty acids — relative to omega-3 — increases low-grade inflammation in your body.

‘Organic’ or ‘Gluten-Free’ Doesn’t Mean Healthy.

Foods that are naturally gluten-free are fine, but gluten-free processed foods are often made with unhealthy ingredients that may even be worse than their gluten-containing counterparts.

The truth is, organic sugar is still sugar and gluten-free junk food is still junk food.

Don’t Blame New Health Problems on Old Foods.

These foods have been a part of the human diet for thousands of years — whereas these health problems are relatively new.
